ABOUT

Hello! My name is Onur Akın Akbal. I graduated from Dr. Kemal Naci Ekşi Anatolian High School in 2019 and recently earned my bachelor’s degree in Chemistry from Yıldız Technical University, Davutpaşa Campus. Throughout my undergraduate studies, I actively participated in research groups and student organizations, including the YTU Astronomy Club, YTU Space Physics Research Group, YTU Eren Research Group, and Aksuvital Quality-Control Team. Within the Astronomy Club, I contributed to projects such as HORIZON and KATAY, where I gained hands-on experience in telescope construction and earthquake-related research. Later, through the Space Physics Research Group, I engaged in scientific activities focusing on ionospheric and space phenomena. For my undergraduate thesis, I worked in the Eren Research Group, studying the functionalization and antibacterial properties of chitosan biopolymers. I also completed my compulsory internship at Aksuvital in the quality-control department, where I gained practical knowledge of GMP standards and pharmacopoeia while working with analytical techniques such as HPLC, GC-MS, GC-FID, FTIR, AAS, and UV-Vis spectroscopy. Beyond chemistry, I am passionate about technology and coding. I enjoy working with HTML, CSS, and Python as hobbies, which complement my scientific background with creative and digital problem-solving skills. My professional interests include polymers, quality control, and instrumental analysis, while my personal hobbies range from amateur astronomy, physics, and biology to PC games, TCG card games, cooking, and continuous learning. I consider myself a curious and quick learner, eager to contribute to research, laboratory work, or related fields while continuing to grow professionally.

KATAY

In the KATAY Project, which aims to identify the factors causing ionospheric anomalies associated with large earthquakes between 2010 and 2020 and investigate how these anomalies can be used in earthquake predictions, we examined the changes in the ionosphere before, during, and after the earthquakes. During my time as a support team member in this project, I had the opportunity to develop my skills in research, literature review, and reading academic papers.

HORIZON

In this project, we aim to research different telescope models, produce our own reflecting telescopes and deliver these produced telescopes to village schools. During my time as a member of the Optics Team in this project, I took part in polishing phase of grinded telescope glasses.
